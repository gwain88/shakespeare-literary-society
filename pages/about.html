About the Shakespeare Literary Society

We are the world's oldest literary society devoted to Shakespearemania. We are the organization responsible for producing the famed and notorious "Shakespeare Scenes," the annual, free, public performance at the Poet's Walk in New York's Central Park.

Founded on April 23, 1864 in a secret location, the Society is the oldest Shakespearian group dedicated to fostering enthusiasm for William Shakespeare's work.

We are devoted to the promotion of the great and the one and only William Shakespeare and his plays and poetry.

Our devotion to William Shakespeare is matched only by our anonymity and secrecy. It will come as no surprise, then, that we do not communicate with the general public.
